# Reflection: “Cloud Architect” Event

### Event Objective

- To provide an engaging platform for learning and knowledge sharing through competitive gameplay.
- To strengthen understanding of Cloud computing and architecture design principles.
- To foster networking and collaboration among interns and peers from different teams.

### Event Format

- **Teams:** 8 teams, each consisting of 5 members.
- **Rules:** Competitive 1v1 rounds with questions ranging from basic to advanced levels.
- **Sudden Death:** In case of a tie, the team that answers the 11th question faster wins.

### Special Tactical Skills

- **Minimal Risk (1 use):** Used for uncertain answers. No point deduction if incorrect; 0.5x points if correct.
- **Hope Star (1 use):** Used for confident answers. 2x points if correct; -2x points if incorrect.

### Key Learnings

#### Architecture Design Mindset
- Understanding how various Cloud services interact to form a cohesive system.
- Recognizing the importance of architecture choices over manual coding.
- Evaluating trade-offs between performance, cost, and system complexity.

#### Risk Management & Decision Making
- Developing the ability to make calculated decisions under time pressure, similar to real-world production environments.

### Practical Applications

- **Architecture Optimization:** Applying cloud knowledge to improve the scalability of current backend projects.
- **Infrastructure Management:** Utilizing a "cloud architect" mindset when managing Docker environments and cloud servers.
- **Portfolio Enhancement:** Integrating system-level design insights into GitHub projects to boost internship application profiles.

### Personal Experience

Participating in the **“Cloud Architect”** event was an exhilarating experience that transformed complex Cloud concepts into exciting, fast-paced challenges.

#### Key Highlights
- **Gamification:** Learning through competition significantly improved my retention of technical concepts.
- **Real-time Decision Making:** Balancing the use of "Hope Star" provided valuable insight into the pressure of making critical architectural decisions.
- **Networking:** Collaborating with peers outside my usual team expanded my technical perspective.

#### Core Takeaways
- System design is a balance between optimization, security, and scalability.
- Clear communication and effective teamwork are essential to overcoming complex technical hurdles.
